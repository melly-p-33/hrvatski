Grammar
Key rules to help you understand how Croatian works
Singular Nouns

Croatian nouns have three genders: masculine, feminine, and neuter. You can usually tell the gender from the ending of the word.

Masculine — most nouns ending in a consonant
grad (city), brod (boat), stol (table), pas (dog)

Feminine — most nouns ending in -a
žena (woman), kuća (house), rijeka (river), knjiga (book)

Neuter — most nouns ending in -o or -e
more (sea), dijete (child), selo (village), ime (name)

⚠ Some nouns break the pattern — tata (dad) ends in -a but is masculine. When in doubt, learn the gender with the word.

Plural Nouns

Plurals change based on gender.

Masculine — add -i
grad → gradovi (cities), stol → stolovi (tables), brod → brodovi (boats)

Feminine — drop -a, add -e
žena → žene (women), kuća → kuće (houses), knjiga → knjige (books)

Neuter — drop -o/-e, add -a
more → mora (seas), selo → sela (villages), ime → imena (names)

⚠ There are irregular plurals you just have to learn — dijete (child) → djeca (children) is a common one Sanja will cover.

Pronouns

Croatian pronouns change based on their role in the sentence. Here are the basic subject pronouns — the ones you use when saying who is doing something.

jaI
tiyou (informal)
onhe
onashe
onoit
miwe
viyou (plural or formal)
onithey (mixed or male group)
onethey (female group)

⚠ Croatian often drops the pronoun entirely because the verb ending tells you who the subject is — so just radim means "I work" without needing ja. You'll still hear and use them for emphasis.

Common Verbs — Present Tense

The forms you'll use most in conversation: ja (I), ti (you), on/ona (he/she).

biti — to be
jasamI am
tisiyou are
on / onajehe / she is
imati — to have
jaimamI have
tiimašyou have
on / onaimahe / she has
ići — to go
jaidemI go
tiidešyou go
on / onaidehe / she goes
raditi — to work
jaradimI work
tiradišyou work
on / onaradihe / she works
živjeti — to live
jaživimI live
tiživišyou live
on / onaživihe / she lives
studirati — to study
jastudiramI study
tistudirašyou study
on / onastudirahe / she studies

⚠ Notice the pattern: -m for ja, -š for ti, no ending for on/ona. Once you see it, it clicks.

Days of the Week

Croatian days are not capitalised unless they start a sentence.

ponedjeljakMonday
utorakTuesday
srijedaWednesday
četvrtakThursday
petakFriday
subotaSaturday
nedjeljaSunday

💡 To say "on Monday" use u ponedjeljak. To say "on Mondays" (regularly) use ponedjeljkom.

Months of the Year

Months are also lowercase in Croatian.

siječanjJanuary
veljačaFebruary
ožujakMarch
travanjApril
svibanjMay
lipanjJune
srpanjJuly
kolovozAugust
rujanSeptember
listopadOctober
studeniNovember
prosinacDecember

💡 To say "in April" use u travnju — months take the locative case with u.
